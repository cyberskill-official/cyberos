# ai-strategy@1 — Annual AI strategy

An `ai-strategy@1` artefact authors the annual AI strategy — use-case portfolio prioritization, build-vs-buy-vs-partner, model governance, training-data strategy, MLOps maturity, EU AI Act risk classification of in-scope use cases, AI ethics posture, budget. Per NIST AI RMF 1.0 + EU AI Act Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 + ISO/IEC 42001:2023 + Stanford HAI policy framework.

## Required sections (template.md H2 order)
1. AI ambition & guiding principles
2. Use-case portfolio (per-use-case: business value × feasibility × risk)
3. Build / buy / partner per use case (with rationale)
4. Model governance posture (model card discipline, version control, drift monitoring)
5. Training-data strategy (acquisition / labeling / licensing / privacy)
6. MLOps maturity (CIS AI/ML / Google MLOps maturity levels)
7. EU AI Act risk classification per use case (prohibited / high-risk / limited-risk / minimal-risk)
8. AI ethics posture (bias / explainability / human oversight)
9. Budget envelope
10. AI OKRs for the year
11. Risks & open issues

## Citations
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)
- EU AI Act Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 risk classification
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (AI management system)
- Stanford HAI policy framework
- Google MLOps maturity model
- Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy (as a reference industry pattern)

## KPI
- Use-case time-to-production
- Model-drift incident count
- AI-related cost / ROI per use case
