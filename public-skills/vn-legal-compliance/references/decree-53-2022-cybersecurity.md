# Nghị định 53/2022/NĐ-CP — Cybersecurity Law implementing decree

**In force:** 1 October 2022.
**Implements:** Cybersecurity Law (Luật An ninh mạng) of 2018.
**Issuing authority:** Government of Vietnam.
**Supervisory authority:** Ministry of Public Security (MoPS), with operational support from Ministry of Information and Communications (MoIC) for telecom carriers and content platforms.

Decree 53 is the long-awaited implementing decree for the 2018 Cybersecurity Law. Where Decree 13 (PDPD) governs personal data, Decree 53 governs **information that affects national security**, including the corresponding data-localisation and local-presence regimes.

## Scope (Articles 1–3)

Applies to:

- Domestic and foreign organisations providing services in Vietnam over telecommunications networks, the internet, or value-added services.
- Especially: telecom service providers, internet service providers, hosting providers, social-network operators, payment intermediaries, e-commerce platforms, OTT messaging, app stores, and cloud service providers.

Out of scope:

- Purely offline systems.
- State-managed national-security systems (separate regulation).

## Data-localisation requirement (Article 26)

Foreign service providers (and certain domestic operators) MUST store the following categories of data on servers physically located within Vietnam:

1. Personal data of users in Vietnam.
2. Data generated by users in Vietnam (account information, billing data, friend lists, social-graph data, posts, comments).
3. Other data prescribed by the Prime Minister.

The retention period MUST be **at least the duration of operations in Vietnam** plus 24 months after closure.

This requirement is triggered for a foreign provider when **all** of the following conditions are met:

- The service is provided in Vietnam.
- The service has Vietnamese users.
- MoPS has issued a formal localisation order — i.e. localisation is "compelled" rather than "automatic". As of 2026, MoPS has selectively issued orders to large social platforms and OTT messengers; many foreign operators have not yet received an order.

## Local-presence requirement (Article 26.3)

Foreign service providers ordered to localise data MUST also establish a **branch or representative office** in Vietnam. The branch:

- Is the local legal-process recipient.
- Cooperates with MoPS on investigations.
- Is liable for compliance failures.

Some foreign operators have negotiated to satisfy this via a designated Vietnamese subsidiary rather than a new branch; the rule is enforced flexibly in practice.

## Content takedown windows (Article 26.2)

When MoPS, MoIC, or another competent authority issues a takedown notice for content that:

- Threatens national security, public order, or social safety;
- Constitutes propaganda against the State or incites violence;
- Defames an organisation or individual;
- Violates intellectual-property rights;

the operator MUST act within:

- **24 hours** for state-security or public-order content.
- **48 hours** for IP infringement and defamation, unless a court order specifies otherwise.

The takedown obligation extends to suspending the offending account and preserving evidence for an MoPS investigation.

## Cooperation obligations (Articles 28–30)

Service providers MUST:

- Designate a 24/7 cybersecurity point of contact.
- Provide MoPS with technical access to user account information upon valid legal request, within the timelines set in the request (typically 24–72 hours).
- Maintain logs of user activity per MoPS technical specifications, retained for a minimum prescribed period (typically 12 months).
- Report cybersecurity incidents to MoPS within 24 hours of detection.

## Critical Information Infrastructure (Article 23)

Operators of "critical information infrastructure" — banking core systems, payment switches, telecom backbones, healthcare records, government IT, etc. — face heightened requirements:

- Security plan filed with MoPS and updated annually.
- Annual third-party security audit.
- Disaster-recovery plan with cross-region replication inside Vietnam.
- Personnel-screening obligations for staff with privileged access.

## Penalties

- Administrative fines per Decree 15/2020 — up to **70 million VND** per violation for organisational respondents.
- **Suspension of service** in Vietnam for repeated or severe violations.
- **Criminal liability** under Article 288 of the Penal Code for the most serious violations involving distribution of prohibited content or unauthorised access to critical infrastructure.

## Practical compliance checklist

1. Determine whether you are a foreign service provider with Vietnamese users (likely if you have any consumer or SME footprint in VN).
2. Anticipate, but do not preemptively trigger, the localisation order — many providers operate without a formal order for years.
3. Designate the 24/7 contact.
4. Wire the 24h takedown SLA into your trust-and-safety runbook.
5. Establish log-retention per MoPS specifications.
6. If you operate critical infrastructure (especially in banking / payments / health), engage local counsel early; the Article-23 regime is materially more onerous.

## Interaction with Decree 13 (PDPD)

Decree 13 and Decree 53 overlap on personal data. The practical rule:

- For **routine personal-data processing**, Decree 13 governs (consent, subject rights, TIA).
- For **state-security-relevant operations**, Decree 53 governs (localisation, local presence, takedown).
- A single operator commonly falls under both. There is no "pick one" — comply with the union.

## Disclaimer

This summary is informational. Consult Vietnamese legal counsel before relying on it for any compliance posture. The decree is published in the Official Gazette (Công báo); MoPS issues operational guidance via circulars.
