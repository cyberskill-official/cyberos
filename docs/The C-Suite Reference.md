## 1. Executive Landscape: What Changed in 2025–2026

The C-suite is undergoing its largest structural shift since the digital revolution. IBM's 2026 CEO Study (2,000+ orgs) reports **76% of organizations now have a Chief AI Officer (CAIO), up from 26% in 2025** — a near-tripling in 12 months. But a counter-signal is equally important: executive recruiters at True Search report that of their last 20 AI-related searches, only **one** was C-level — meaning AI fluency is rapidly being absorbed into existing CIO/CTO/CDO/CISO mandates rather than spawning a standalone permanent seat. Treat CAIO as a transitional role: essential now, likely embedded by 2028.

Other macro signals from Harvard Law's 2026 NEO study (Russell 3000): **CLOs recorded the largest absolute increase** of any non-CFO role 2021–2025; **CTOs jumped from 155 to 249 disclosures**; CHRO and CCO (Commercial) rose; **business-unit-head NEOs fell 15%** — power is centralizing into functional Chiefs. CEO tenure hit a record low of 6.8 years; CRO tenure averages 17–25 months (volatile); COO tenure ~3.2 years; CLO tenure ~6.1 years (most stable).

**Rising:** CAIO, CLO, CTO, CHRO (as Chief Human Transformation Officer), CRO (Revenue), Chief Trust/Ethics/Privacy, Chief of Staff. **Declining/merging:** Chief Metaverse Officer (effectively extinct since 2023), Chief Diversity Officer (politically pressured in US), traditional CMO (often absorbed into CRO or Chief Customer Officer), standalone Chief Sustainability Officer in some markets. **Merging:** CIO+CHRO into "Chief People & Technology Officer" (life-sciences precedent, 2025); CISO+CPO-Privacy; CTO+CPO-Product in <100-person firms.

## 4. The Universal Persona Template (apply to every role)

Every AI persona CyberSkill builds should follow this nine-block schema:

1. **Identity & scope** (disambiguated title, reports to, reports in, prevalence %)
2. **Information inputs** (data, dashboards, reports, market intel they consume)
3. **Stakeholder inputs** (CEO/Board mandates, peer-C-suite asks, customer/regulator signals)
4. **Resource inputs** (budget envelope, headcount, tooling)
5. **Outputs** — strategic (roadmaps, plans), operational (decisions, approvals), communication (board decks, all-hands), team (hires, culture)
6. **Cadence** — daily / weekly / monthly / quarterly / annual
7. **KPIs** — 3–5 quantitative metrics
8. **Audit criteria** — quantitative thresholds + qualitative rubric + failure modes
9. **Tools & stack**

Below, every role is rendered in compressed form using this schema.

## 5. Role Profiles

### 5.1 Operations & Strategy

**CEO (Chief Executive Officer)** — Highest authority, sets vision, allocates capital, owns board relationship. Inputs: board mandates, market intel, financials, customer signals, exec team reports. Outputs: company strategy, OKRs, capital allocation, key hires, external narrative. KPIs: revenue growth, EBITDA, market cap/valuation, employee NPS, board confidence. Audit red flags: missed quarters with no narrative, exec churn, capital misallocation, founder-mode lock-in past 100 employees. Tools: board portals, OKR systems, exec dashboards.

**COO (Chief Operating Officer)** — Owns execution; bridges strategy to delivery. Tenure shortest at 3.2 yrs. Inputs: CEO priorities, dept reports, ops metrics. Outputs: operating model, process design, SLAs, cross-functional alignment. KPIs: on-time delivery, gross margin, ops efficiency ratios, throughput. Failure modes: role ambiguity vs CEO, becoming "CEO's cleanup crew" without authority. Tools: ERP, BI, EOS/OKRs, process mining.

**CSO (Chief Strategy Officer)** — Long-horizon thinking, M&A, market positioning. Inputs: market research, competitive intel, board scenarios. Outputs: 3–5 yr strategy, M&A theses, market-entry plans. KPIs: strategy-execution alignment, M&A IRR, market-share moves.

**CGO (Chief Growth Officer)** — Cross-functional growth across marketing/sales/product. Often evolves into CRO. KPIs: CAC, LTV:CAC, growth efficiency, NRR.

**CAO (Chief Administrative Officer)** — Back-office aggregator (facilities, admin, sometimes HR/IT in older firms). Increasingly merged or eliminated.

### 5.2 Finance & Legal

**CFO (Chief Financial Officer)** — Owns FP&A, capital, controls, investor relations; modern CFOs co-own data and tech investment decisions (Deloitte 2025). Earns 37–39% of CEO comp. Inputs: GL, forecasts, market data, board asks. Outputs: budgets, forecasts, board financials, capital plan, IR narrative. KPIs: forecast accuracy (±5%), cash runway, gross/net margin, working capital, audit clean. Failure modes: forecast drift >10%, control weaknesses, slow close (>10 days). Tools: NetSuite/SAP, Adaptive, Anaplan, Stripe data, BI.

**CLO (Chief Legal Officer / General Counsel)** — Fastest-growing NEO role 2021–2025. Owns legal, regulatory, contracts, litigation, governance. Often absorbs Compliance in mid-market. KPIs: litigation exposure, contract cycle time, regulatory finding count, M&A close quality.

**CRO (Chief Revenue Officer)** — Owns sales + marketing + customer success as one engine. Triggers at $10–20M ARR. McKinsey: Fortune 100 firms with CRO-equivalents see 1.8× peer revenue growth. KPIs: ARR, NRR (>110%), CAC payback (<18 mo), LTV:CAC (>3), pipeline coverage (3–4×), win rate. Failure modes: tenure under 24 months, sales/marketing silo persistence, hero-deal dependence.

**Chief Accounting Officer** — Controller-level, owns GAAP/IFRS, audit, SOX. Reports to CFO. Quiet but essential pre-IPO.

### 5.3 Technology, Data & AI

**CTO (Chief Technology Officer)** — Outward-facing: product architecture, engineering, tech roadmap. NEO disclosures rose 155→249 (2021–2025). Inputs: product strategy, customer feedback, tech-trend intel. Outputs: tech vision, architecture, build-vs-buy, engineering org. KPIs: deployment frequency, change-failure rate (<15%), MTTR, engineering velocity, recruiting ratio.

**CIO (Chief Information Officer)** — Inward-facing: enterprise IT, internal tooling, data infra, cybersecurity oversight. Often co-owns AI investments with CTO/CFO (Deloitte). KPIs: system uptime (>99.9%), IT spend as % revenue, internal NPS, incident MTTR.

**CISO (Chief Information Security Officer)** — Cyber risk owner. Often reports to CIO/CEO/CRO-Risk. KPIs: mean time to detect/respond, vuln-closure SLA, audit findings closed, phishing-test pass rate. Vietnam context: increasingly required under Decree 13/2023 on Personal Data Protection.

**CDO (Chief Data Officer)** — Data strategy, governance, analytics, increasingly AI. KPIs: data-quality scores, time-to-insight, model deployment count, data-product revenue.

**CAIO (Chief AI Officer)** — 76% prevalence in 2026 (IBM). Owns AI strategy, governance, scaling. Transitional role — likely absorbed by CTO/CDO/CIO within 3 years. KPIs: # AI use cases in production, % workforce regularly using AI (industry baseline 25%), AI-driven cost reduction, AI risk incidents = 0.

**Chief Product Officer (CPO-Product)** — Owns the "why/what/when"; CTO owns the "how." Customer-centric strategy, roadmap, pricing, market fit. KPIs: NPS, activation, retention, feature adoption, time-to-value.

**Chief Architect / Chief Software Architect** — Below CTO in most firms; rarely C-level except at infrastructure-heavy enterprises.

### 5.4 Marketing, Revenue & Customer

**CMO (Chief Marketing Officer)** — Pressure: many CMO roles being absorbed into CRO or Chief Customer Officer; the "death of the CMO" narrative is overstated but role is narrowing toward brand+demand. KPIs: brand awareness, MQL→SQL conversion, CAC, share of voice.

**CCO (Chief Commercial Officer)** — Sales + partnerships + sometimes marketing; common in B2B services. KPIs: bookings, win rate, deal size.

**Chief Customer Officer** — Owns post-sale CX, success, support. KPIs: NRR, churn, CSAT, NPS, expansion revenue.

**CXO (Chief Experience Officer)** — End-to-end experience across product + service. Overlaps CCO/CMO.

**Chief Sales Officer (CSO-Sales)** — Pure sales leadership; often reports to CRO.

**Chief Brand Officer** — Brand stewardship; rare standalone, usually within CMO.

**Chief Communications Officer** — PR, IR, internal comms, crisis. Common at enterprise.

### 5.5 People & Culture

**CHRO / CPO-People** — Now repositioned as **"Chief Human Transformation Officer"** (Bersin, WEF, IMD 2026). 91% of CHROs rank AI as top concern (CHRO Association). Four AI-era roles: design architect, capability steward, adoption catalyst, transition guardian. KPIs: voluntary attrition (<10%), eNPS, time-to-fill, internal mobility %, AI fluency % of workforce, training hours/FTE.

**CDO (Chief Diversity Officer)** — Under political pressure in US; rebranding as "Inclusion & Belonging" or merging into CHRO. Still strong in EU/APAC.

**Chief Learning Officer** — Reskilling owner; increasingly central in AI era. KPIs: skill coverage, internal-fill rate, certification rates.

**Chief Happiness Officer** — Novelty/branding role; substance overlaps with CHRO. Useful as a culture signal, not a budget center.

### 5.6 Risk, Compliance, Trust, Ethics

**CCO (Chief Compliance Officer)** — Designs compliance management system; personal liability rising. KPIs: regulatory findings = 0, training completion (>95%), policy currency, incident closure SLA.

**CRO (Chief Risk Officer)** — Enterprise risk framework; risk appetite vs tolerance. Different from CCO: CRO sets *how much* risk; CCO ensures *legality*. KPIs: KRI breach count, capital adequacy, scenario-test coverage.

**Chief Privacy Officer (CPO-Privacy)** — GDPR/CCPA/Vietnam Decree 13 owner. KPIs: PIA coverage, breach notification timeliness, DSR response time, training completion.

**Chief Trust Officer** — Emerging meta-role spanning security + privacy + ethics + transparency, especially in SaaS where customer trust is the product.

**Chief Ethics Officer / Chief AI Ethics Officer** — Emerging; oversees responsible-AI policy, bias audits, model cards. KPIs: ethics-review coverage, bias-test pass rate, AI incident count.

### 5.7 Sustainability, Transformation, Specialty

**Chief Sustainability Officer (CSO-Sustain)** — ESG reporting (CSRD/ESRS in EU), Scope 1/2/3 emissions, sustainable supply chain. KPIs: emissions reduction, ESG ratings, regulatory disclosures clean.

**Chief ESG Officer** — Variant overlapping CSO-Sustainability.

**Chief Digital Officer** — Digital transformation; often time-bounded (3–5 yrs) then sunsets into CIO/CTO.

**Chief Transformation Officer** — Cross-functional change leadership for specific programs (M&A integration, restructuring).

**Chief Innovation Officer** — R&D portfolio, new-venture incubation. KPIs: innovation pipeline value, % revenue from new products.

**Chief Knowledge Officer** — Rare except in consulting; manages organizational learning assets.

**Chief Procurement Officer (CPO-Procurement)** — Supply chain + vendor management at scale.

**Chief Investment Officer (CIO-Invest)** — Asset-management firms; portfolio strategy.

**Chief Medical Officer** — Healthtech/insurtech only.

**Chief Restructuring Officer** — Crisis-only; appointed during distress/turnaround, often interim.

**Chief Automation Officer** — Emerging in ops-heavy firms; usually absorbed by CIO/CTO.

**Chief Remote Officer** — Peaked 2020–2022, now embedded in CHRO.

**Chief Metaverse Officer** — Effectively extinct; cautionary tale on naming roles after hype cycles.

**Chief of Staff** — Not strictly C-level but operates at it. CEO leverage multiplier; rhythm-of-business owner; OKR/decision tracker; special-projects lead. Visible.vc and First Round consensus: one of the most important early hires. KPIs: CEO time reclaimed, on-time decision closure, OKR adoption, cross-functional initiative delivery.

## 6. Audit Criteria Framework (apply to any C-role output)

**Quantitative gates:** Does the output move the role's 3 primary KPIs? Is forecast/plan within ±10% of actuals? Are leading indicators (not lagging) being tracked?

**Qualitative rubric (1–5 per dimension):** (a) Alignment with CEO/board mandate; (b) Internal coherence with peer-C-suite plans; (c) Customer/market grounding; (d) Risk-adjusted realism; (e) Communicability — can a non-expert understand it?

**Universal failure modes:** (1) Playbook transplant — applying a prior-company tactic without diagnosing context; (2) Activity over outcomes — measuring meetings/touches not results; (3) Silo-ing — outputs that ignore adjacent functions; (4) Forecast drift without narrative; (5) Hero dependence — performance tied to one person; (6) AI-washing — claiming AI value without measurable production use cases.

**Differentiation by output type:** *Strategic* outputs audited on 3-yr horizon coherence; *operational* on cycle time and defect rate; *cultural* on lagging eNPS + leading behavior-signal data.

## 7. CyberSkill-Specific Application

CyberSkill is a Vietnamese software consultancy founded 2020, currently in scale-up phase. Recommended C-suite trajectory:

**Now (≤50 people):** Stephen as CEO; technical co-founder/CTO; **Chief of Staff** as next critical hire (operating-system owner, OKR cadence, founder leverage); fractional CFO; outsourced legal, security, privacy. Don't title anyone "Chief X" who isn't truly C-level — prospeo/a16z warn this creates re-leveling pain past 25 people.

**Series A / 50–100 people:** Promote CTO formally; hire **Head of Delivery** (the consulting-firm equivalent of COO — owns utilization, margin, client satisfaction); hire **Head of People** (precursor to CHRO). Add **VP Sales/Head of Growth**, not yet CRO.

**100–200 people (scale-up):** Hire full CFO; elevate Delivery to COO; hire CHRO; hire **Chief Revenue Officer** to integrate sales+marketing+account-management (especially critical for global expansion from Vietnam — managing time-zone-spanning revenue engines benefits from unified ownership). Appoint a **vCISO** (virtual CISO) given Decree 13/2023 and global client SOC2/ISO27001 demands. Add **Chief Privacy Officer** function (can be part-time legal counsel).

**200+ / Global enterprise:** Full suite: CEO, COO, CFO, CTO, CPO-Product, CRO, CHRO, CLO, CISO, CDO/CAIO. Consider **Chief Customer Officer** (consulting firms live on NRR). A dedicated **Chief Knowledge Officer** is unusually high-ROI for consulting firms — institutional IP is the moat.

**Consulting-firm-specific structures:** Unlike pure SaaS, consulting needs (a) **Practice Leaders / Partners** with P&L ownership at the C-1 level; (b) **Delivery COO** distinct from corporate COO; (c) **Chief Knowledge / IP Officer** to codify reusable assets; (d) **CRO with services-revenue fluency** (different KPIs: utilization × bill rate × realization, not just ARR).

**Vietnam context:** Hierarchy expectations are stronger than US norms — titles carry signaling weight with clients and talent; but be cautious about over-titling early hires. For global client work, Western-standard titles (CTO, CRO, COO) translate better than localized variants. Comply with Decree 13/2023 (personal data) — privacy function is not optional for clients with EU/US users.

## 8. Commercial Use — Client Baseline Heuristics

When advising clients, CyberSkill should anchor on five rules:

1. **Don't add a C-title to solve a process problem** — if a Head-of can do it, don't promote the problem.
2. **Match the title to the funding stage** (use Section 3 matrix).
3. **Disambiguate every acronym in writing** in the offer letter and org chart (Section 2).
4. **Beware hype-cycle titles** (Metaverse, even pure CAIO long-term) — design roles around durable verbs (build, secure, monetize, govern) not nouns-du-jour.
5. **Audit outputs, not titles** — use Section 6 framework regardless of name on the door.

For each AI persona CyberSkill builds, instantiate Section 4's nine-block schema, parameterize against the relevant client's stage from Section 3, and constrain the persona's tools/KPIs to the role profile in Section 5. This produces specific, auditable, and culturally portable C-suite agents — internal personas that operate at executive caliber, and commercial baselines clients can adopt with confidence.
